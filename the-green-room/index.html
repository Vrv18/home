Portkey Announcement Video // Final Script

The Green Room

A 90-second video that introduces Portkey to the US tech ecosystem. The premise: A CTO calls an emergency meeting because nothing broke.

Runtime 90-112 seconds
Distribution LinkedIn, X
Audience CTOs, Platform Engineers, Architects
Tone Margin Call meets controlled absurdity
Tagline AI that never breaks.

Full Script

The Hook

0-3s
Office hallway. Fire alarm BLARING. Red lights flashing. People rushing.
Fire alarm sound.
3-6s
They rush toward conference room. Through glass: THE CTO (50s, suit jacket, no tie) standing calmly, holding a small metal dustbin. It's on fire.
6-9s
He sets it down. People file in, confused. He gestures for them to sit.
9-12s
CTO: "There is no fire."

The Setup

12-14s
He lets that land. Turns laptop toward them. On screen: dashboard. ALL GREEN.
14-18s
CTO: "This week, we pushed AI into three new product lines. Twelve teams. Four million users."
18-21s
Beat. He looks around the room.
CTO: "Explain to me what went wrong."
21-24s
Silence. People exchange glances.
24-27s
ENGINEER 1 (30s, hoodie) speaks carefully.
ENGINEER 1: "Nothing... went wrong."
27-30s
The CTO holds on him.
CTO: "Nothing."
30-32s
ENGINEER 1: "Nothing."

The Interrogation

32-36s
The CTO sits. Folds his hands. Measured, not angry.
CTO: "Then someone explain why my entire AI infrastructure is running perfectly and I don't know how."
36-40s
ENGINEER 2 leans forward.
ENGINEER 2: "We put a gateway in the middle."
40-42s
CTO: "A gateway."
42-48s
ENGINEER 2: "It handles the failovers. The retries. The caching. Sixteen hundred models. We don't touch it anymore."
48-52s
The CTO's tone sharpens slightly. Still controlled.
CTO: "You handed the keys to our entire AI infrastructure to a vendor I haven't vetted?"
52-55s
Beat. Engineer 1, calm, not defensive:
ENGINEER 1: "Yes."
55-57s
The CTO waits for more.
CTO: "Who is the vendor?"
57-59s
ENGINEER 1: "Portkey."
59-62s
Beat.
CTO: "Never heard of them."

The Line

62-67s
Engineer 1 meets his eyes. Simply:
ENGINEER 1: "That's the point." (beat) "You don't hear about the things that don't break."
67-70s
The CTO holds. No comeback. Looks at green dashboard. Then back at them.
Silence.

Validation

70-74s
Engineer 2, filling silence with facts, not defensiveness:
ENGINEER 2: "They processed eighty trillion tokens last year. Ninety-three million in API spend. Lightspeed and Elevation just backed them."
74-76s
ENGINEER 1: "They're built to not break. It's like Cloudflare, but for models."

The Release

76-79s
The CTO exhales. A small nod. Almost to himself.
CTO: (quieter) "So we built something that just... works."
79-81s
Beat. He looks at the team.
CTO: "So what do you do now?"
81-84s
Engineer 1, small smile:
ENGINEER 1: "Build more things, I guess."
84-87s
The CTO nods. The faintest warmth.
CTO: "Then get out of my conference room."

Button #1: The Real Fire

They start to stand—
89-91s
THE FIRE ALARM BLARES AGAIN.
Alarm sound.

Everyone freezes. The CTO's eyes light up — finally, a real problem?
93-95s
They turn toward glass wall. Outside: ACTUAL FLAMES. Real fire. Different building.
Silence under alarm.
95-97s
The CTO, deadpan:
CTO: "Now that's a real problem."
97-102s

Different location. Casual. The two FOUNDERS sit watching this ad on a laptop. It ends.
Ambient room tone.

FOUNDER 1: "Think he'll remember our name?"
108-110s
Founder 2 shrugs.
110-112s

SUPER: PORTKEY — portkey.ai

Key Lines

"There is no fire."
Hook payoff — the emergency is the absence of emergency
"Twelve teams. Four million users. Explain to me what went wrong."
Stakes — CTO knows what should have broken
"We put a gateway in the middle. It handles the failovers, the retries, the caching... we don't touch it."
Technical credibility — specific but accessible
"You handed the keys to our entire AI infrastructure to a vendor I haven't vetted?"
Real tension — this is what a CTO would actually say
"Never heard of them."
Perfect setup — earns the skepticism the audience shares
"You don't hear about the things that don't break."
THE LINE. This is the ad. Everything else is setup for this moment.
"It's like Cloudflare, but for models."
Instant positioning — borrows trust from known infrastructure
"Build more things, I guess." / "Get out of my conference room."
Emotional release — warm, human, earned
04

Characters

THE CTO
50s, male, suit jacket no tie. Authoritative but not loud. Skeptical but not hostile. The reasonable one asking hard questions.
Energy: Controlled intensity. Never yells. Never mugs. The audience surrogate.
ENGINEER 1
30s, hoodie. The one who made the call to use Portkey. Has to defend the decision.
Energy: Calm confidence. Not nervous. Not arrogant. Delivers the killer line.
ENGINEER 2
30s, slightly more polished. The technical explainer who provides the "how."
Energy: Matter-of-fact. Provides stats and technical details.
THE FOUNDERS
Portkey founders. Casual, self-aware. Appear only in the button scene.
Energy: Human, warm, self-deprecating. The shrug is genuine.
05

Production Notes

The Burning Dustbin (0-9s)

  • Small metal office trash can, controlled flame (paper burning)
  • CTO holds it calmly, sets it down like it's nothing
  • Visual absurdity: fire alarm for a tiny controlled fire

The Dashboard

  • Clean, modern observability UI (Datadog/Grafana feel)
  • Unnaturally, completely green — no yellow, no red
  • Should feel almost unsettling in its perfection

The CTO's Physicality

  • Sits when asking questions — not slamming tables
  • Folds hands. Leans back. Controlled.
  • "Get out" delivered with faintest smile — warmth, not dismissal

The Real Fire (93-97s)

  • Visible through glass wall — another building, actual flames
  • Surprising but not alarming — a visual punchline
  • "Now that's a real problem" is dry, not panicked

Founders Button (102-112s)

  • Completely different energy from main ad
  • Casual setting — actual office, couch, somewhere human
  • The shrug is genuine — not a bit, just real

The Core Dynamic

The absurdity isn't in the CTO — it's in the situation. He's the straight man. The comedy comes from the gap between his earned skepticism and the engineers' unbothered calm.
